Short Biography

I am a population health scientist at UT-Austin's Population Research Center. Currently, I am a postdoctoral fellow on the DeCodE Zika and Covid project, which is dedicated to studying the impact of infectious disease epidemics on maternal and infant outcomes in Brazil. More broadly, I specialize in combining multiple complex data sources—such as birth records, medical insurance claims, and social services data—to investigate a broad range of topics in maternal and child health epidemiology. I ask questions related to:

  • The prevalence and effectiveness of targeted prenatal care services (Does Wisconsin's Prenatal Care Coordination program prevent low birth weight? Is there county-level variation in the program's uptake?)
  • Sociodemographic factors and their relations to infant health and childhood development (Does socioeconomic deprivation amplify shorter gestation's harm on children's early literacy skills?)
  • The validity of common measures in maternal and child health epidemiology (Can we rely on birth records for identifying Medicaid-paid deliveries?)
  • Identifying health-related spillover effects in families (How do we isolate and measure spillover effects between siblings in the presence of unobserved confounding?)

I earned my PhD in Population Health Sciences from the University of Wisconsin-Madison. In my dissertation, I built a grahpical model for identifying spillover effects within sibling pairs (i.e., how one person's treatment affects their sibling's outcome—Annals of Epidemiology manuscript here). I then applied the model to estimate the effect of shorter gestation on older sibling's kindergarten-level literacy and to estimate the effect of Wisconsin's Prenatal Care Coordination program on children's receipt of preventive care services.


News

January 25, 2022: I recently published a paper, "Estimating sibling spillover effects with unobserved confounding using gain-scores," in the Annals of Epidemiology. In this paper, we proposed a novel approach for measuring exposure-to-outcome spillover effects in sibling pairs while controlling for bias from unobserved sibling-invariant confounding. This work was coauthored with Felix Elwert (University of Wisconsin). Link here: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.annepidem.2021.12.010.
